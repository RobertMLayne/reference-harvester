Patent Trial and Appeal Board

Local
571-272-9797
Fax
571-273-0053
Kalyan Deshpande
Acting Chief Judge for the Patent Trial and Appeal Board

The Patent Trial and Appeal Board (PTAB) is a tribunal within the United States Patent and Trademark Office (USPTO) that reviews rejections made by examiners in ex parte appeal proceedings and adjudicates patentability disputes regarding issued patent claims challenged by third parties in trial proceedings under the America Invents Act (AIA). 

The AIA created the PTAB (also called the Board). The Board was known previously as the Board of Patent Appeals and Interferences (BPAI). In fact, the Board has a long history (under different names) going back to the 1860s.

The Board includes four members designated by statute, along with the administrative patent judges (APJs). The statutory members of the Board are: the USPTO Director, the Deputy Director, the Commissioner for Patents, and the Commissioner for Trademarks. 

APJs are appointed by the Secretary of Commerce and are required to have both legal and technical training. APJs have extensive patent legal experience prior to their appointment on the Board, for example, in private practice, in government practice (e.g., at the USPTO, Department of Justice, or the International Trade Commission), and as in-house counsel at companies. Many APJs have also served as judicial law clerks or USPTO patent examiners. 

Additionally, the Board includes patent attorneys, paralegals, and support staff who help to administer proceedings and hearings.

Administrative patent judges
 

Administrative patent judges are appointed by the Secretary of Commerce and are legally and technically trained. Judges have extensive patent legal experience prior to their appointment on the Board, for example, in private practice, in government practice (e.g., at the Department of Justice or the International Trade Commission), and as in-house counsel in companies. Many also have served as USPTO examiners or judicial law clerks.
